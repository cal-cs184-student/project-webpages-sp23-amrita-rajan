Part 4. Depth of Field

For these subparts, we recommend using a microfacet BSDF scene to show off the cool out of focus effects you can get with depth of field!

In a few sentences, explain the differences between a pinhole camera model and a thin-lens camera model.

With a pinhole camera model, everything is in focus. When we shoot a ray in the direction (x, y, -1), the image receives it at (-x, -y, 1). The image will only receive radiance from (0, 0, 0) with this model. On the other hand, with the thin-lens camera model, only objects that are within focal distance are in focus. The origin of the ray will be sampled from the lens and it will be in the direction of the plane of focus. It will have the same intersection as the ray from the pinhole camera since rays with the same point on the plane of focus will have the same focused image point.
